Overview of the CT Hurst Museum Archaeological Collection

The CT Hurst Museum serves as a center of object-based learning and a resource for Western Colorado University students, faculty, staff, and the Gunnison Valley community to explore human history of the region. The archaeological collection at the CT Hurst Museum contains items that provide significant opportunities for students and staff to learn about the geographic, cultural, historic, prehistoric, and natural environment of the Western Slope, with a specialized focus in the Gunnison Basin.

The CT Hurst Museum is primarily interested in collections from the cultural, historic, prehistoric, and natural environment of the Western Slope, with a specialized focus in the Gunnison Basin. Records and artifacts are accepted with the understanding that the primary usage is research by students, scholars, and other authorized persons. Although the acceptance of collections is primarily limited to the Western Slope, The CT Hurst Museum will consider accepting collections from other areas when these collections benefit the overall research goals of the students and faculty of the Anthropology Program.

Mission Statement

The CT Hurst Museum Archaeology Collection is a steward of the material culture and natural history of the Western Slope. As an academic museum and repository located on the campus of Western Colorado University, the museum is a catalyst for education through the exhibition, research, preservation, and administration of the collection. The CT Hurst owns, acquires, and cares for its collections on behalf of the public it serves.
